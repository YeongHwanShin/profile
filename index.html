Golden Capsule

Non-powered medicine injector for disaster relief (2023)

Project Overview

Role: Lead Designer, Researcher
Timeline: March 2023 - November 2023
Team: 5 members (Yeonghwan Shin, Daeyeon Kim, Yujin Chae, Yuan Bai, Jiho Kim)
Tools: Fusion 360, Unity, Arduino, Ansys Discovery

Challenge

Emergency medical situations in disaster zones require quick medication delivery without traditional power sources. How might we design a reliable, non-powered injection system that works in extreme conditions where infrastructure is compromised?

Solution

Designed a non-powered medicine injector utilizing air pressure and elasticity. The device requires no external power source and can deliver precise medication doses in emergency situations. By focusing on fundamental physics principles, we created a reliable, fail-safe system that works independently of electricity or batteries.

Impact & Recognition

James Dyson Award International Winner (2023)
Capstone Design Fair Prime Minister Winner - 1st Place (2023)
Creative Engineering Design Award, MMR Workshop (2023)
Prototype for Humanity - Health, Relief and Safety Winner (2023)
Published in IEA Triennial Congress 2024
Exhibited at Seoul Design Festival, Design Korea, Red Dot Design Museum

Reflection

This project taught me the importance of designing for extreme constraints. By focusing on the fundamental physics of air pressure and elasticity, we created a solution that works reliably without batteries or electricity—critical for disaster relief scenarios. The international recognition validated our user-centered approach to humanitarian design.

Minwon 99

Malicious civil complaint filtering system (2024)

Project Overview

Role: AI/ML Developer, UX Researcher
Timeline: 2024
Tools: LLM-based Text Emotion Moderation, Python, Machine Learning

Challenge

Civil servants face an increasing volume of malicious complaints that consume valuable time and resources. How might we use AI to filter and moderate emotionally charged complaints while maintaining fairness and transparency?

Solution

Developed an LLM-based text emotion moderation system that automatically identifies and filters malicious civil complaints. The system uses natural language processing to analyze emotional content and intent, helping civil servants prioritize legitimate concerns.

Impact & Recognition

Best Paper Prize at HCI Korea 2025
Published in Proceedings of HCI KOREA 2025
Co-authors: Seong-min Choi, Hohyun Song, Kyusung Lee, Eunji Park

SophyBARA

Philosophical debate AI agents (2025)

Project Overview

Role: Research Lead, AI Developer
Timeline: 2025 (Ongoing)
Tools: Multi-agent systems, RAG, n8n, GPT-4

Challenge

How might we use AI agents to simulate philosophical debates between historical thinkers, providing users with diverse perspectives on complex ethical and existential questions?

Solution

Developing a multi-agent system where AI agents embody different philosophers' thinking styles. Users can pose questions and watch philosophical debates unfold, offering educational value and critical thinking training through exposure to diverse viewpoints.

Current Status

Active research project exploring multi-agent systems in XR environments. Planning expert evaluation with philosophy professors and usability testing with general users.

Paw Pulse

AI pet health monitoring system (2025)

Project Overview

Role: UX Researcher, AI Developer
Timeline: 2025
Recognition: XREAL XR Hackathon 2nd Winner

Challenge

Pet owners struggle to monitor their pets' health and detect early signs of illness. How might we leverage AI and XR technology to provide accessible health insights?

Solution

Developed an AI-powered pet health monitoring system that helps owners track and understand their pets' wellbeing. The system provides actionable insights and early warning signs for potential health issues.
